London

How to reach

  1. By Air:-

    2. London is served by six international airports: Heathrow Airport, Gatwick Airport, London City Airport, Luton Airport, Stansted Airport and Southend Airport. Together, they make up the busiest airspace in the world by passenger numbers.

  2. By Train:-

    3. St Pancras railway station is the main terminal for high speed Eurostar and High Speed 1 services linking London with Europe.

Getting Around

  1. Bicycle:-

    2. Santander Cycles is a bike-share system with hundreds of bicycle docking stations that are located across London.Visitors can hire a bike using a bank card at the docking station or by downloading the app.

  2. Train:-

    3. The Tube, London’s Underground rail network services Greater London between 5a.m. and midnight Monday to Saturday, with reduced operating hours on Sunday. There are five Tube lines that run a 24-hour service on Fridays and Saturdays: Victoria, Central, Jubilee, Northern and Piccadilly lines. More information about the network and fares can be found online.

  3. Bus:-

    4. London’s bus route network services the city on a 24-hour service. More information about the network and fares can be found online.

  4. Taxis and Ridesharing:-

    5. In London it is the best to find an official taxi stand. You can also book a taxi online or on your smartphone. Uber, Bolt and Kapten could also be the prefered choices.

  5. Trams:-

    6. South London operates a small tram network called "Tramlink", with trams running apx. every 10 minutes in the daytime on Mondays to Saturdays.
