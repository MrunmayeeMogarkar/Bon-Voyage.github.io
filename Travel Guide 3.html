Paris

How to reach

  1. By Air:-

    2. Paris is served by two international airports: Paris-Charles de Gaulle (the second busiest airport in Europe) and Paris-Orly.

  2. By Train:-

    3. The Gare du Nord is a major transit hub for trains to Northern France and to international destinations in Belgium, Germany, the Netherlands, and the United Kingdom.

Getting Around

  1. Bicycle:-

    2. Tourists can purchase or rent bicycles based on one- or seven-day subscription from Vélib'. Vélib' is a bike-share system with thousands of bicycle stationed across Paris and in some surrounding municipalities. Subscriptions can be purchased at either the docking stations or online.

  2. Train:-

    3. RATP operates its metro and RER services daily.These services operate from 5:30 a.m. to about 1:15 a.m, and on Fridays and Saturdays until 2:15 a.m. The metro system is the city’s local underground subway network which services central Paris and the RER stands for the regional express network. More information about the network and fares can be found online.

  3. Bus:-

    4. RATP also operates Paris’ local bus system daily from 5:30 a.m. to about 1:15 a.m and on Fridays and Saturdays until 2:15 a.m. At night, the Noctilien night bus network takes over, operating 47 bus lines from 12:30 a.m. to 5:30 a.m. More information about the network and fares can be found online.

  4. Taxis and Ridesharing:-

    5. In Paris it is the best to find an official taxi stand. You can also book a taxi online or on your smartphone.Uber and Bolt could also be the prefered choices.

  5. Scooters:-

    6. Electronic scooters are available on rent through an app called "Cityscoot" on your smartphone.
