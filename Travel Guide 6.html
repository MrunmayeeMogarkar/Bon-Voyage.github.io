Prague,Czech Republic

How to reach

  1. By Air:-

    2. Prague Ruzyne Airport (Václav Havel Airport Prague) has direct flights to the US, Canada, and many European cities. Airport Express buses and public bus 119 connect the airport to the city.

  2. By Train:-

    3. Prague Central Train Station (Praha Hlavni Nadrazi) has trains to cities around the Czech Republic and to Central Europe destinations like Vienna, Budapest and Bratislava.

Getting Around

  1. On Foot:-

    2. Prague’s central neighborhoods are easy to get around on foot. It’s possible to visit most attractions without needing to using public transport.

  2. Bicycle:-

    3. Prague is not the most bike-friendly city, but cycling is becoming more popular.Prague has an electric bike-share system called "Freebike" with bicycle stations that are located across Prague. More information on fares and bicycle stations can be found online.

  3. Train:-

    4. DDP operates metro and tram services daily from 5 a.m. to 12:30-1 a.m. Tickets can be bought from the automated ticket machines at metro and tram stations. Day passes and single fares are available. Tickets are also valid for the Petrin funicular. More information about the network and fares can be found online.

  4. Bus:-

    5. Prague’s local bus system operates day and night. However, most travelers find it quicker and more convenient to take the tram. More information about the network and fares can be found online.

  5. Taxis and Ridesharing:-

    6. It’s possible to hail down a taxi on the street in Prague, but prices will generally be higher. It is also advisable to agree on a fare before you set out. Alternatively, just find an official taxi stand or get your hotel to call one for you. Uber and Bolt could also be the prefered choices.

  1. Charles Bridge
  2. Old Town Hall with Astronomical Clock
  3. Staromestske Namesti
  4. Prague Castle
  5. Petrin Lookout Tower
