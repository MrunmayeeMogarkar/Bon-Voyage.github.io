Old Town Hall with Astronomical Clock

Old Town Hall became an administrative centre of Old Town in Prague in 1338, when the estates acquired approval of King John of Bohemia to establish it. The clock was placed at the top of the tower in 1805. The astronomical clock is made up of three parts: 1. At the top, there are mechanical figures. 2. Every hour from 9.00 a.m. to 11.00 p.m., 12 apostles, appear in the two windows below the small roof. Each one of them carrying his attribute. In the left window, viewed from the visitors, the first to appear is St. Peter with a key, then St. Matthew with an axe, St. John with a chalice, St. Andrew with an X-shaped cross, St. Philip with a cross. and St. James with a washboard. In the right window, viewed from the visitors, the first to appear is St. Paul with a book, then St. Thomas with a spear, St. Simon with a saw, St. Thaddeus with boards, St. Bartholomew with leather, St. Barnabas with a scroll.
