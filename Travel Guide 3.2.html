Eiffel Tower

The Eiffel Tower is a wrought-iron lattice tower in Paris. It is located on the Champ de Mars. It is named after the engineer Gustave Eiffel.It was constructed from 1887 to 1889 as the entrance to the 1889 World's Fair. It was initially criticised by some of France's leading artists and intellectuals for its design. Now it has become a global cultural icon of France and one of the most recognisable structures in the world.The Eiffel Tower is the most-visited paid monument in the world; almost seven million people visit the Eiffel Tower annually and about 300 million visitors have been to the tower since its opening.The tower is 324 metres tall, about the same height as an 81-storey building, and is also the tallest structure in Paris. Its base is square, measuring 125 metres on each side. During its construction, the Eiffel Tower become the tallest man-made structure in the world by surpassing the Washington Monument.It was a title that the Eiffel Tower held for 41 years until the Chrysler Building in New York City was finished in 1930. It was the first structure to reach a height of 300 metres. Becuse of the addition of a broadcasting aerial at the top of the tower in 1957, the Eiffel Tower is now taller than the Chrysler Building by 5.2 metres.
