Zaanse Schans

A cultural heritage village on the Zaan, The Zaanse Schans is a unique village in the Netherlands, full of wooden houses, windmills, barns and workshops. The Zaanse Schans is a living-working area in which the 18th and 19th centuries revive. You get a chance to stroll past the bakery museum and enjoy the smell of fresh cakes, or take a look inside the warehouse where clogs are made. There is also a model cheese factory, tin foundry and the various mills. You can take a bike or boat trip, browse the shops or enjoy the pancake restaurant. A day out on the Zaanse Schans in North Holland is fun and educational.The Zaanse Schans attracts nearly a million visitors per year and it's one of the Netherlands' best loved attractions. The outdoor portion of the Zaanse Schans is open to view without paying admission, but opening times and entrance prices vary at each of its museums, workshops, and windmills.

Dive into Dutch history and learn more about how people used to live. Learn how raw materials are processed, how products are made and how people used to transport them. Operate the machine yourself to wrap chocolate in its wrapper or dive into the world of watchmaking at Museum Zaanse Tijd. Do you visit several museums? Then you save a lot with the Zaanse Schans Card . The card is for sale at the information center in the Zaans Museum, the Wevershuis and at Museum Zaanse Tijd.
